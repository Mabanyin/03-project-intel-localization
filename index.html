Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental initiatives.

2023

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, helping to drastically lower carbon emissions and driving progress toward Intel's long-term sustainability goals.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

Intel RISE Strategy
Responsible

Intel takes responsibility for its global impact by setting science-based targets for greenhouse gas reduction, investing in renewable energy, and working to reduce their carbon footprint across all operations.

Inclusive

Creating a more inclusive workplace and technology industry through diverse hiring practices, equal pay initiatives, and programs that support underrepresented groups in STEM fields.

Sustainable

Building products and processes that minimize environmental impact through water conservation, waste reduction, circular economy practices, and sustainable manufacturing techniques.

Enabling

Using technology as a force for good by developing innovations that help solve global challenges, from climate modeling to accessible computing for people with disabilities.

2030 Goals: Intel aims to achieve net positive water use, zero waste to landfill, and 100% renewable electricity across global operations.
Net-Zero by 2040 Commitment
The Commitment

In 2022, Intel announced its ambitious goal to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across its global operations (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040. This commitment represents a comprehensive approach to decarbonization.

Key Strategies
  • Energy Efficiency: Implementing advanced technologies to reduce energy consumption in manufacturing and facilities
  • Renewable Energy: Transitioning to 100% renewable electricity worldwide
  • Green Chemistry: Developing and using environmentally friendly chemicals in production
  • Carbon Capture: Investing in carbon abatement technologies
Progress Tracking

Intel publishes annual sustainability reports with transparent metrics on emissions reduction, renewable energy adoption, and progress toward 2040 goals. The company works with third-party auditors to verify its environmental data.

Milestone: By 2023, Intel achieved 99% renewable electricity usage globally, a major step toward the 2040 net-zero goal.
Water Conservation & Waste Reduction
Water Stewardship

Intel's water conservation efforts focus on three key areas: reducing water use, restoring watersheds, and ensuring access to safe water in communities where Intel operates.

  • Conservation: Advanced water recycling systems that reuse water multiple times in manufacturing
  • Restoration: Partnering with local organizations to restore and protect watersheds
  • Net Positive Goal: Returning more water to communities than Intel uses by 2030
Circular Economy & Waste

Intel implements circular economy principles to minimize waste and maximize resource efficiency throughout the product lifecycle.

  • Zero Waste: 90% of hazardous and non-hazardous waste diverted from landfills
  • Material Recovery: Recycling precious metals and materials from manufacturing
  • Product Design: Creating products that are easier to recycle and upgrade
  • Packaging: Using sustainable packaging materials and reducing waste
Impact: Intel conserves billions of gallons of water annually and has restored over 4 billion gallons of water through watershed restoration projects.
